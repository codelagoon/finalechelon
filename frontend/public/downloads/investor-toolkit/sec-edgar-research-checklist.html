How to Find Company Filings

Visit SEC EDGAR Database
Go to sec.gov/edgar and search by company name or ticker symbol
Locate Company Search
Find the company's CIK (Central Index Key) and access their filing history

10-K Filings (Annual Reports)

Find the Most Recent 10-K
Locate the latest annual report (filed after fiscal year-end)
Item 1: Business
Read the business description to understand what the company does
Item 1A: Risk Factors
Review all risk factors—these are critical for understanding downside
Item 7: Management's Discussion & Analysis (MD&A)
Read management's narrative on financial performance and outlook
Item 8: Financial Statements
Review the audited financial statements and notes

10-Q Filings (Quarterly Reports)

Find Recent 10-Qs
Locate the most recent quarterly reports for updated financial data
Part I: Financial Information
Review quarterly financial statements and MD&A updates
Part II: Other Information
Check for legal proceedings, risk factor updates, and controls

What Sections to Read First

1. Business Description (Item 1)
Understand the company's products, markets, and competitive position
2. Risk Factors (Item 1A)
Identify key risks that could impact the investment thesis
3. MD&A (Item 7)
Read management's explanation of results and future outlook
4. Financial Statements (Item 8)
Analyze the income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement
5. Notes to Financial Statements
Review notes for accounting policies, contingencies, and segment data

Key Items to Extract

Revenue Trends
Track revenue growth over 3-5 years and identify growth drivers
Margin Analysis
Calculate gross, operating, and net margins over time
Capital Structure
Understand debt levels, interest coverage, and financial flexibility
Cash Flow Generation
Analyze operating cash flow and free cash flow trends
Segment Information
Review revenue and profitability by business segment/geography

Red Flags to Watch For

Declining Margins
Consistent margin compression may indicate competitive pressure
Rising Debt
Increasing leverage ratios may signal financial stress
One-Time Gains
Be skeptical of earnings boosted by non-recurring items
Accounting Changes
Watch for changes in accounting policies that may affect comparability
Related Party Transactions
Review related party disclosures for potential conflicts of interest
